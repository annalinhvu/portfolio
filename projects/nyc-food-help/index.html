I need a hot meal today

Soup kitchens and community meals serve food on-site — no cooking required. Most are open daily with no registration needed. Many are welcoming to everyone, no documentation required.

🛒
I need groceries

Food pantries give you bags of groceries to cook at home. Mobile pantries bring food directly to your neighborhood. Many use a client-choice model — you pick what your family will actually eat.

🏠
I cannot travel

Home delivery programs bring food to your door if you're unable to leave due to illness, disability, or age. Senior meal programs serve adults 60+ at community centers or via delivery. A brief call to confirm eligibility is usually required.

I need help by phone

Call 311 and ask for emergency food assistance or food pantries. Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
For SNAP benefits help, call NYC HRA at 718-557-1399.
For national help, call the USDA National Hunger Hotline at 1-866-3-HUNGRY.

Before you go
✓Call ahead — hours change without notice, even at well-established programs.
✓Many programs have specific intake days or require appointments. Check the organization's website first.
✓Bring ID if you have it, but most programs don't require documentation.
✓Services are for everyone. Most NYC food programs do not require income verification.
✓If a site is full or closed, call 311 for alternatives near you.

Understand your options

Not sure what type of organization fits? A plain-language guide by what you need.

🍲 I need a meal today
🍲
Soup Kitchen
A warm meal right now

Hot meals served on-site — usually breakfast, lunch, or dinner. No cooking needed. Most open daily with no registration required.

Good if you need to eat today, no strings attached.
🏘️
Community Meal
Neighborhood-run meals

Hot food hosted by churches, community centers, or neighborhood groups — usually on specific days. Welcoming to everyone, no ID required.

Good if you want a community atmosphere alongside a meal.
🛒 I need groceries
🛒
Food Pantry
Take groceries home

Bags of groceries to take home and cook. Many use a "client-choice" model — you pick what your family will actually eat.

Good if you have a kitchen and want to cook for your household.
🚚
Mobile Pantry
Food comes to your neighborhood

Food trucks or pop-up markets bring groceries to neighborhoods. Schedules rotate — check each organization's website for the current schedule.

Good if you can't travel far, or there's no pantry nearby.
🏠 I cannot travel
🏠
Home Delivery
Food delivered to your door

Food brought to you if you can't leave due to illness, disability, or age. Requires a brief call to confirm eligibility.

Good for people with serious illness, disability, or mobility challenges.
👵
Senior Meals
For adults 60 and older

Congregate meals at community centers, or home delivery. Often paired with social activities and benefits counseling.

Good for adults 60+ who want meals and community connection.
🤝 I want to volunteer
♻️
Food Rescue
Redistributing surplus food

Food rescue groups collect surplus from restaurants, stores, and farms and redistribute it through partner pantries, shelters, and meal programs.

Good to know: food rescue groups often supply partners — they may not be direct walk-in sites.
🤝
Volunteer opportunities
How you can help

NYC food organizations need volunteers for sorting, serving, food rescue pickups, and delivery support. Many welcome first-timers with no experience required.
