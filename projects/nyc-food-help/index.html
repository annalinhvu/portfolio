Resource guide

Understanding your options

Not sure which type of organization is right for you? Here's a plain-language guide.

🛒
Food Pantry
Take groceries home

A food pantry gives you bags or boxes of groceries to take home and cook yourself. Many use a "client-choice" model — you shop like in a store, choosing foods your family will actually eat.

Good if you have a kitchen and want to cook for your family.
🍲
Soup Kitchen
A warm meal right now

Soup kitchens serve hot meals you eat on-site — usually breakfast, lunch, or dinner. No cooking or kitchen required. Most are open daily with no registration needed.

Good if you need to eat something today, no strings attached.
🚚
Mobile Pantry
Food comes to your neighborhood

Mobile food trucks or pop-up markets bring food directly to neighborhoods. No need to travel far. Locations and times rotate — check the organization's website for the current schedule.

Good if there isn't a pantry near you, or you can't travel easily.
♻️
Food Rescue
Rescued from restaurants and farms

Food rescue organizations collect surplus food from restaurants, grocery stores, and farms that would otherwise go to waste. They redistribute it as free meals or groceries.

Good if you want fresh, restaurant-quality food at no cost.
🏠
Home Delivery
Food delivered to your door

Home delivery programs bring food directly to you if you're unable to leave due to illness, disability, or age. Usually requires a brief phone call to apply and confirm eligibility.

Good for people with serious illness, disability, or mobility challenges.
👵
Senior Meals
For older adults

Many programs specifically serve adults 60 and older, either at community centers (congregate meals) or delivered at home. Often paired with social activities and benefits counseling.

Good for adults 60+ who want meals and community connection.
